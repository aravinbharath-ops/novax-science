Certificate of Analysis: A Practical Research Guide

Understand what a Certificate of Analysis contains, how to read it and how it supports research-material traceability.

2026-07-17 · 6 MIN READ

What is a Certificate of Analysis?

A Certificate of Analysis, commonly called a COA, is a batch-specific document summarising analytical information for a material. It may include identity, purity, testing method, batch number and the date of analysis.

Why batch matching matters

A COA is useful only when it can be connected to the exact labelled batch. Researchers should compare the batch reference on the container with the identifier shown in the verification record.

What to review

Review the product name, batch identifier, reported test method, result, testing date and issuing laboratory. Any mismatch should be clarified before the material is introduced into a research workflow.

Digital verification

A secure verification system adds another traceability layer by connecting a unique code to the original product record and the corresponding documentation where available.
